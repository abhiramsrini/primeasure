Why it matters: Virtual ads and augmented graphics now ride on IP-first production chains. Chyron’s Virtual Placement 8.0 adds native SMPTE 2110 ingest/output and tighter camera tracking, removing SDI-only constraints and helping broadcasters monetize courtside inventory faster.

What’s new in Virtual Placement 8.0

  • 2110 end-to-end: Uncompressed IP ingest/output lets graphics stay in the ST 2110 domain without SDI roundtrips, aligning with modern REMI/centralized control rooms.
  • Hardware-free court tracking: Computer-vision tracking for basketball removes physical sensors, shrinking setup time and on-site kit.
  • Expanded sport layouts: Prebuilt court templates speed calibration and ad placement for multiple basketball venues.
  • Lower-latency preview: Faster scene render/preview improves operator confidence during live tosses.

Recommended workflow

  1. Bring 2110 camera feeds into Virtual Placement 8.0; keep PTP clock alignment consistent with your production network.
  2. Use the basketball templates to lock calibration; validate with a short pan/tilt path and sideline replay.
  3. Publish clean/dirty outputs over 2110 for your vision mixer; reserve one multiviewer tile for operator preview.
  4. For monetization, prebuild sponsor looks (baseline, center-court, benches) and map them to MOS/automation cues.

Implementation checklist

  • Confirm ST 2110 support on your backbone (bandwidth, NMOS control, PTP).
  • Stage a latency test: capture glass-to-glass with and without AR to prove timing before match day.
  • Document a fallback scene (minimal graphics) to cover tracking dropouts.
  • Align ad ops with legal on virtual inventory zones for each venue.
