Why Cloud Production Now?

Grass Valley recently explored whether cloud-native production is the breakthrough sports broadcasters have been waiting for. Their article, “Is Cloud Production the Game-Changer Sports Broadcasting Has Been Waiting For?” outlines both the promise and the practical hurdles. Primeasure supports Indian broadcasters making the same transition, so we distilled the highlights and added local context.

Benefits Grass Valley Highlights

  • Operational flexibility: Spin up resources as events demand, rather than keeping OB vans idle between tournaments.
  • Talent leverage: Remote production allows specialists to contribute from anywhere, reducing travel costs and fatigue.
  • Resilience: Cloud platforms provide redundancy and global reach—vital for rights holders serving international audiences.
  • Innovation velocity: Updates and new features can roll out without waiting for hardware refresh cycles.

Readiness Checklist for Indian Broadcasters

  1. Assess connectivity. Ensure reliable low-latency links from stadiums to your preferred cloud region, with secondary paths for backup.
  2. Plan security and compliance. Align with data residency and sports league requirements before sending contribution feeds to the cloud.
  3. Integrate legacy systems. Decide which routing, graphics, and replay tools will stay on-premises and how they’ll interoperate with cloud services.
  4. Invest in training. Grass Valley notes the need for cultural change—operators must be comfortable mixing, switching, and monitoring from software interfaces.

Primeasure Perspective

We recommend starting with marquee events or secondary channels to build confidence. Hybrid deployments—where contribution is cloud-based but distribution stays on-prem—help mitigate risk while preparing teams for full adoption.
