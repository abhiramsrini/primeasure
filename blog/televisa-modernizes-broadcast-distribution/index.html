How Televisa Modernized Broadcast Distribution with Synamedia MEG

22 November 2025 4 min read Primeasure Interpretation

Why Televisa Needed a New Distribution Core

Televisa’s legacy satellite and fiber workflows made disaster recovery slow and costly. In Synamedia’s case study, the broadcaster outlined gaps in redundancy, switching resilience, and operational visibility. With viewers expecting uninterrupted channels and rapid turnaround for live events, the network needed an IP-first refresh that balanced uptime with predictable costs.

Synamedia MEG Architecture Highlights

  • Multi-path redundancy: MEG’s router-agnostic design let Televisa run fiber, satellite, and internet links concurrently, reducing single points of failure.
  • Centralized control: Unified management gave operators a single view of ingress, processing, and egress health, shrinking mean time to recovery.
  • Protocol flexibility: Support for DVB, ASI, and IP feeds simplified gradual migration without disrupting existing playout stacks.
  • Modular scaling: Software-defined edges enabled incremental capacity adds instead of forklift upgrades.

Business Outcomes Televisa Reported

  1. Improved resiliency. Automatic failover across multiple carriers minimized blackouts during network incidents.
  2. Faster incident response. Operators could isolate and re-route issues in minutes with centralized telemetry.
  3. Lower TCO. Moving to software-driven edge processing reduced reliance on bespoke hardware, trimming CAPEX and ongoing OPEX.
  4. Future-ready distribution. The IP-first foundation prepares Televisa for regional expansion and new channel launches without re-architecting.

Primeasure Perspective for Indian Broadcasters

Many Indian networks face similar constraints: mixed satellite/IP links, aging ASI infrastructure, and stringent uptime SLAs. A MEG-style multi-edge design lets you phase migration by city or channel while retaining legacy contribution feeds. We recommend piloting with secondary channels first, validating automatic failover paths, and then scaling to national distribution.

Implementation Tips

  • Map every ingress/egress path. Document fiber, satellite, and internet routes so failover policies cover all critical channels.
  • Segment monitoring. Use distinct alerting for contribution, processing, and distribution layers to speed root-cause analysis.
  • Test switchover regularly. Schedule drills for planned and unplanned failovers to validate automation and operator playbooks.
  • Align with security teams. Harden IP edges with access control and encryption as you retire closed ASI domains.
